INVESTMENT THEMES

Innovation: New Solutions for Old Problems

We believe there will always be opportunities to equitably transform existing systems for the better, and have dedicated a significant proportion of this strategy to them since inception.

Representative Holdings:

  • Duolingo (DUOL) - The learning app that democratizes language education globally
  • Mercadolibre (MELI) - Latin American e-commerce enabling local merchants to reach bigger markets

Infrastructure: The Rails and Roads that Connect Us

The right infrastructure can make our vast planet feel like one global village. The long-term nature of these businesses can make them excellent investments.

Representative Holdings:

  • Quanta Services (PWR) - Building and maintaining electric power infrastructure for the smart grid transition
  • Vinci SA - Global construction and engineering of essential transport infrastructure

Lending: Banks that Fund Our Future

Progress hinges on the availability of capital. These banks are positively enmeshed in their communities, avoiding predatory practices through strong local linkages.

Representative Holdings:

  • Farmer Mac (AGM) - America's largest agricultural lender supporting rural communities
  • Amalgamated Bank (AMAL) - Built by labor organizers, still at the forefront of social responsibility

Real Estate: Bricks and Mortar that Build Community

Real estate investments tied to positive social systems, serving local communities directly or delivering on practical industry needs.

Representative Holdings:

  • Alexandria Real Estate (ARE) - Lab space provider serving as launchpad for innovative biotech companies
  • Armada Hoffler (AHH) - Developer of mixed-use urban communities in the Mid-Atlantic

Q4 2024 Market Review

Global equity markets delivered solid returns in the fourth quarter, with the MSCI ACWI advancing 2.1%. Technology and healthcare sectors led gains, while energy and materials lagged on concerns about slowing global growth and commodity price weakness.

The portfolio's outperformance was driven by strong stock selection in technology and healthcare, where our focus on companies with sustainable competitive advantages and positive societal impact continues to bear fruit.

Performance Drivers

Key Contributors: Our overweight position in semiconductor companies benefited from continued AI infrastructure buildout. TSMC (+18%) and ASML (+15%) were top contributors as they remain critical enablers of technological advancement.

Key Detractors: Our underweight to traditional energy names cost approximately 20 basis points of relative performance. However, this aligns with our ethical screening process that excludes fossil fuel companies with inadequate transition plans.

Portfolio Positioning

We maintain conviction in companies addressing critical global challenges through innovation. Key overweights include:

• Healthcare Innovation: Companies developing breakthrough therapies and expanding global access to medicine

• Digital Transformation: Enablers of productivity and sustainability through technology

• Sustainable Infrastructure: Firms building the physical and digital infrastructure for a low-carbon economy

Outlook

Looking ahead, we see continued opportunities in companies that generate positive impact through their core business models. While market volatility may persist, our focus remains on identifying high-quality businesses where ethics and excellence converge.

Recent additions to the portfolio include leaders in renewable energy technology and companies pioneering circular economy solutions. We believe these positions align with both our ethical framework and long-term value creation.

INVESTMENT APPROACH

Philosophy

The Global Opportunities strategy seeks long-term capital appreciation by investing in companies worldwide that meet our stringent ethical criteria while demonstrating strong fundamentals and growth potential.

We believe that companies operating ethically and sustainably are better positioned for long-term success, as they face fewer regulatory, reputational, and operational risks while benefiting from growing demand for responsible business practices.

Our Thematic Approach

We organize our research and portfolio construction around four core themes that represent enduring opportunities for positive impact and value creation:

1. Innovation: New Solutions for Old Problems
Companies that equitably transform existing systems through technology, business model innovation, or creative approaches to persistent challenges.

2. Infrastructure: The Rails and Roads that Connect Us
Businesses building and maintaining the physical and digital networks that enable global commerce and community connection.

3. Lending: Banks that Fund Our Future
Financial institutions positively enmeshed in their communities, providing capital for productive purposes while avoiding predatory practices.

4. Real Estate: Bricks and Mortar that Build Community
Real estate investments tied to positive social systems, whether serving local communities directly or enabling critical industry needs.

Our Screening Process

Step 1: Ethical Exclusions
We begin by excluding companies involved in weapons, tobacco, gambling, fossil fuels without credible transition plans, and human rights violations. This typically eliminates 68% of the MSCI ACWI.

Step 2: Thematic Alignment
We evaluate remaining companies for alignment with our four investment themes, seeking businesses where positive impact and revenue generation are intrinsically linked.

Step 3: Fundamental Analysis
Companies undergo rigorous analysis across six dimensions: People, Product, Execution, Valuation, Moat, and Risk. We seek companies with strong fundamentals trading at reasonable valuations.

Step 4: Portfolio Construction
We build a concentrated portfolio of 40-60 high-conviction positions, balancing thematic exposure, geographic diversification, and risk management while maintaining our ethical standards.

Step 5: Continuous Monitoring
Holdings are continuously monitored for both financial performance and ethical compliance. Client input helps shape our evolving screening criteria, making every investor a research partner.

Portfolio Management

The Gardening Approach: We actively seek "weird little flowers" - overlooked companies generating positive impact through their core business model, not as a side effect. Our research process is designed to discover these off-the-beaten-path opportunities that others miss.

Rebalancing: We rebalance portfolios monthly on the new moon, a practice that helps us maintain discipline while acknowledging that investing, like ethics, is as much art as science.

Risk Management: Through our ethical screening and fundamental analysis, we believe we're avoiding lower-quality businesses. Excluded companies often carry hidden risks - regulatory, reputational, or operational - that can destroy value over time.

Note: This strategy is BDS-compliant and excludes companies on the UN database of businesses involved in Israeli settlements. We also exclude companies failing to meet our standards on labor practices, environmental impact, and governance. Full exclusion lists are available at ethicalcapital.com/exclusions
